# Learning to Learn
### Techniques and Strategies to Reach Your Academic Goals

An interactive, evidence-based workshop on how people learn.

Rather than offering generic study tips, this workshop guides participants through a live cognitive psychology experiment and introduces research-backed learning strategies including retrieval practice, spaced repetition, elaboration, dual coding, interleaving, implementation intentions, and the protégé effect.

Built and presented by Jesse Rogers for TRiO students at Palm Beach State College.

## Live Site

[Learning to Learn](https://rogersmath.github.io/trio/)

## Overview

Most students have been taught **what** to learn.

Few have been taught **how learning actually works**.

This workshop introduces students to decades of research from cognitive science and educational psychology through an interactive experience designed to challenge common assumptions about intelligence, memory, and academic success.

The central message is simple:

> Learning is less about who you are and more about what you do.

Participants:

- Experience a live memory experiment.
- Compare shallow and deep processing.
- Explore how experts organize knowledge.
- Learn evidence-based study techniques.
- Develop a personal implementation plan.
- Leave with a concrete strategy to test immediately.

## Workshop Structure

### Act I — The Experiment

Participants are randomly assigned to different processing tasks and then unexpectedly tested on recall.

This recreates the classic **Levels of Processing** finding from Craik & Lockhart (1972), demonstrating that meaningful engagement produces stronger memory than surface-level processing.

### Act II — How Experts See the World

Introduces research on chess expertise and chunking.

Participants explore how experts recognize meaningful patterns rather than memorizing isolated facts.

### Act III — Katherine Johnson & Mental Models

Uses a scene from *Hidden Figures* and an interactive quadratic formula explorer to demonstrate how experts organize knowledge into larger conceptual structures.

### Act IV — The Good News

Connects learning research to student success and introduces the idea that many academic challenges are strategy problems rather than ability problems.

### Act V — The Tools

Five evidence-based learning techniques:

1. Retrieval Practice
2. Spaced Repetition
3. Elaboration
4. Dual Coding
5. Interleaving

### Act VI — Scientist of Your Own Learning

Students develop implementation intentions and commit to testing a strategy in a specific class.

### Act VII — Teach to Learn

Introduces the protégé effect and encourages students to transition from consumers of knowledge to creators and teachers.

## Educational Design Goals

This workshop was intentionally designed to:

- Replace passive listening with participation.
- Demonstrate concepts before explaining them.
- Connect cognitive science to daily academic practice.
- Increase student self-efficacy.
- Encourage experimentation and reflection.
- Promote teaching as a learning strategy.
